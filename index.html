Introduction


Welcome to my portfolio! I am Zonar Caldera, wrapping up my final year in IT-Media Design at Fontys. In the final semester, I will be doing my internship at Henhouse Studio. I have chosen to do a Creative Technology internship, which is slightly different from IT-Media Design. On this website, you will find my Approach and Results for my graduation internship. Since I am following the Creative Technology methodology for my internship, I will be sectioning the website into the following phases:

  • Trigger Phase
  • Ideation Phase
  • Spec & Design Phase
  • Realization Phase
  • Evaluation Phase

Project Context:

The company: Henhouse Studio, an IT-Consultancy agency that also offers creative and innovative IT-solutions. Conceptual innovation is the strongest suit of Henhouse, and they are on a journey to commit to pushing boundaries and embracing sustainable and forward-thinking practices.

The challenge
Together with my own vision and the vision from Henhouse, we set out to work on a proof of concept that will show how generative AI can improve organizational work efficiency by allowing gen AI to take over repetitive tasks. Tasks that can be easily replicated and repeated by an AI. Most organizational workflows are just moving one data point to another. For example, in client onboarding, some tasks require someone to enter or acquire the information of the client that is onboarding.

Stakeholders
The seed for this project was sown by Henhouse Studio's vision of streamlining organizational processes through AI. In a world clinging to outdated methods, we see a golden opportunity to demonstrate the efficiency and creativity AI can bring to the workplace, not by replacing humans but by enhancing our work and creativity.





Creative Technology Methdology

Since this is going to be an explorative and in-depth project, I chose to do the Creative Technology Internship in order to dive into this domain and explore the possibilities within that specific domain. Creative Tech allows me to build and experiment with different technologies and also research those technologies. It is based on triggers that start the project and that will slowly turn into a complete concept. Additionally, it will involve creating a proof-of-concept, rather than a fully working prototype. The proof-of-concepts will be multiple components that can be added to a system, rather than a complete system. This project will be adhering to the Creative Technology phases: Trigger, Ideation, Specifications & Design, Realisation and Evaluation.

Trigger Phase:

This serves as the initial motivation behind the project, where I explain the different types of ideas and concepts that were first pitched when starting this project. This phase also contains my vision for this project, which acts as a trigger for the reason for this project. Additionally, I will hold a brainstorming session about AI Applications and touch upon the current situation at Henhouse, detailing the different technologies and proposed concepts.

Ideation Phase:

In this phase, I will be detailing the research questions that I will be tackling in this project, ranging from how AI can transform organizational workflows to the societal and ethical considerations. This phase also includes the literature study, where I dive into different studies that talk about how Gen AI can transform organizational workflows. After that, I will conduct a comparative analysis of how AI applications are currently using Gen AI for project management. Lastly, I will conduct an interview with the stakeholder to gather more details on how to tackle the development and necessary details for this project.

Spec and Design:

During this phase, I will be showing the different scenarios for these systems that I will be creating, showcasing different use cases. The requirements for these systems are also detailed on this page, showing the different requirements that fit with the scenarios that I sketched. It also highlights how I will be validating the system later on. This phase ends with a development plan, where I go into the technical details on how to approach the development part of this project.

Realization Phase:

In this phase, I show how these systems are created with the tools that I've specified in the trigger phase: n8N, Notion, and the OpenAI API. I have separate documents on each of these, explaining how I approached the tools and how I used them to create the system with the requirements in mind.

This page also contains a video of how the system works and behaves, detailing the information and the agents that I've created for the system.

This page also contains a system diagram, where I explain the workings of the entire system as a whole. I've created a separate document with 4 different levels for a diagram, adhering to the C4 model that details different levels of a system, from a full overview to a single component of the system. Each level is more detailed than the other.

Evaluation Phase:

In the last phase, the Evaluation Phase, I will be examining the criteria that I defined to see if the system adheres to these criteria, primarily focusing on the output to determine if it yields meaningful and useful results. Afterward, I will discuss the results with my stakeholders and co-workers, gathering their feedback and conducting a thorough analysis of how these systems, particularly the component that I created, can enhance organizational workflows.
