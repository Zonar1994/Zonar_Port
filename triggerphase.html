Trigger Phase

Introduction

Goal of Project

The primary goal of this project is to explore how generative AI, particularly large language models (LLMs), can enhance and optimize workflow processes within organizations. The focus is on leveraging AI's capabilities to process and analyze large amounts of information, breaking down complex data and integrating knowledge effectively into everyday work processes

Why this project?

The trigger for this investigation stems from the understanding that generative AI can significantly contribute to organizational efficiency. Specifically, LLMs have the potential to not only streamline information processing but also dissect and augment knowledge within various workflows. Moreover, recognizing that many organizational processes are repetitive and time-consuming, this project seeks to determine whether AI can reduce the duration and improve the execution of these repetitive tasks. This exploration is timely and relevant, as it aligns with the broader need for innovative technological solutions that can enhance productivity and creativity in workplace settings.

This involves using generative AI to create content and perform tasks based on available information and self-devised strategies, tailored to users/employees' needs.
Our strategy is divided into four main areas: suggestions of elements, reverse engineering, personas, and task execution.

    Proposed Concepts

    Attribute Extender: This tool is designed as a brainstorming partner to help with ideation and experimentation. It generates and suggests properties for various value streams or projects. For instance, when working on developing a Persona, the Attribute Extender can suggest attributes that align well with it, aiding employees in their creative processes.


    Reverse Engineering:This concept focuses on dissecting projects into actionable tasks. These tasks can be assigned and executed by either AI agents or humans, streamlining project management and execution. If implemented effectively, it has the potential to significantly reduce the time spent on project management and increase operational efficiency.
